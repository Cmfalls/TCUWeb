The Edison Institute is a project of The Carbon Underground, the Center for Regenerative Agriculture and Resilient Systems at California State University, and the Schuman Family Foundation.

Lead Partner / Strategic Anchor
The Carbon Underground

Since 2014, TCU has been a leader at the intersection of climate change and agriculture, connecting farmers, food companies, researchers, and governments to advance soil health and draw down legacy carbon.

CRARS at CSU Chico

The Center for Regenerative Agriculture and Resilient Systems is among the most respected institutional experts on soil health and its foundational importance for climate resilience. Their team are "farmers who are also scientists", ensuring every finding is grounded in practical reality.

Est. 1887 Role / Research
Founding Philanthropic Partner
Schuman Family Foundation

Their extraordinary commitment, more than seven million dollars in land and operating support, created the physical and financial foundation for the Edison Institute. Their donation of the 320-acre Edison Farm, secured by a permanent restrictive covenant, ensures this land will serve scientific research and regenerative agriculture in perpetuity.
