Chapter 01 / The Mechanism
How It Works

We don't draw the carbon down. Nature does.

Nature uses photosynthesis to draw excess carbon, our old emissions, back down into the soil to feed the billions of hungry organisms in every teaspoon of healthy soil.

But after two centuries of Industrial Agriculture, our soil is not healthy, and not drawing the excess carbon back down, causing climate change.

The good news is that we can restore soil health with Regenerative Agriculture.

Chapter 02 / The Consequence
Legacy Carbon

Legacy carbon is already shaping farm and water decisions.

The climate conversation often stops at emissions cuts, but the atmosphere is already carrying the carbon load that is intensifying drought, heat, flood risk, and ecological disruption.

Some of that burden can remain in the system for centuries. Restoring living systems will not replace the energy transition, but it does address part of the damage that is already overhead and already affecting land.
